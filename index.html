Digital Divide

By Colton, Abdi, and Betelhem

What is Digital Divide?

The use of and, subsequently, dependence on modern technology in our everyday lives have changed not only how people interact with one another (e.g., digital technology), but also how cities operate. “Smart cities”, or urban areas that use information and communication technologies for operational efficiency, utilize cameras and sensors— among other tools— to collect data and use insights for urban planning and development. However, the systematic implementation of new and improved technologies as a result do not always meet and satisfy the various needs of city residents. In this project, we will reflect on how different “smart” technologies disproportionately impact smart city residents— particularly low-income residents— such that inequitable access to and utilization of technology have broader social implications and lead to other disparities; this is known as digital exclusion.



Technology Advances

Modern technology has become so ingrained in everyday life and normalized in society that it is difficult to imagine the existence of technological disparities. Digital exclusion, otherwise known as the digital divide, has far-reaching and long-lasting consequences for those that are impacted— especially for those from low-income backgrounds— such that the lack of access to and utilization of technology limits their life opportunities.

Seattle's growth as a tech hub

Seattle is growing as one of the main tech hubs in the states. Seattle is the nation's fastest growing tech hub. According to GeekWire, “No city has added more tech jobs in the last two years than Seattle.” New reports show that companies in San Francisco and other hubs continue to flock to the pacific northwest to take advantage of its top-tier talent.

Smart City

As Seattle transitions to a smart city, citizens of the Seattle metro area will experience technology designed to improve their quality of life, while having the opportunity to meet the growing demand for jobs in the tech industry. According to an analysis by CBRE real estate, “Seattle brought in more than 48,000 tech jobs from 2016 to 2020, bringing the total number of tech employees in the Seattle metro area to 184,662.”

Technological Progress

Technology have been growing significantly in the past years. A technological change involves the invention of technologies and their release as open source via research and development, the continual improvement of the technologies, and the diffusion of the technologies throughout the industry or society.

Seattle's boom Tops Silicon Valley

Seattle shows no signs of slowing its progress as it climbs to many of the top economic and quality of life ratings in the country. As the booming tech and medical sector continue to grow and support other tourist and business ventures, it very well may become the new Silicon Valley. With all its recreation and culture.

The Internet

The internet has shows advancement throughout the years. Today living without the internet is almost impossible. Once considered a luxury, high-speed internet is now found in roughly three-quarters of American households. The web served a key role in making the internet popular among the general public and in developing the boundless trove of information that most people can access on daily basis. No matter how far away you are from your friends and family, you can easily stay connected with them and be a part of their lives.
